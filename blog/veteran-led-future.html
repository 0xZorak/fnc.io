A Veteran-Led, Self-Sustaining Future

March 2025

Executive Summary

Five Nines Consulting (FNC) is a veteran-led initiative designed to provide military veterans with a structured pathway to career success, homeownership, and entrepreneurship. By combining hands-on labor, technical education, and community building, FNC ensures that highly skilled veterans from combat arms, Special Operations, Airborne, and the Marine Corps transition seamlessly into meaningful civilian roles while maintaining a strong sense of purpose, discipline, and camaraderie.

FNC is more than a training program; it is a self-sustaining, veteran-led community. Veterans will work 20 hours per week on practical projects such as construction, woodworking, IT infrastructure, and sustainable farming, while studying 12 hours per week in the FNC remote learning center. Those who excel will be given the opportunity to continue developing their skills remotely while working for FNC. The program also features an on-site modular data center, connected to FNC headquarters via a dedicated fiber loop, ensuring secure operations, training, and job placement in IT and cybersecurity.

Furthermore, FNC offers affordable homeownership programs, where veterans apply for a home built by their peers. Veterans must assemble a team or pay fellow veterans to build their home, utilizing materials and additional funding from FNC. Once completed, FNC sells the home at cost to the veteran, ensuring affordable housing without mortgage traps.

Mission Statement

To provide elite veterans with the skills, resources, and community needed to achieve self-sufficiency, career success, and homeownership, ensuring they continue their legacy of leadership, discipline, and innovation beyond their military service.

Core Pillars of Five Nines Consulting

1. Veteran Selection & Structured Training

  1. Initial selection will be highly competitive, focusing on combat arms, special operations, airborne, and Marine Corps veterans.
  2. Veterans will work 20 hours per week on practical hands-on projects such as:
    1. Construction & welding
    2. Mechanics & vehicle repair
    3. Forestry, woodworking, and furniture making
    4. Hydroponics & aquaponics for year-round food production
    5. On-site data center infrastructure
  3. Veterans will study 12 hours per week in the remote learning center, focusing on:
    1. IT, networking, and cybersecurity
    2. Business and entrepreneurship
    3. Leadership and operations management
  4. Successful graduates will be given remote work opportunities with FNC to further develop their IT and business skills.

2. On-Site Data Center & IT Infrastructure

  1. A modular data center will be built on-site to support:
    1. Veteran IT training and remote work opportunities
    2. Secure communications and decentralized data management
    3. AI-driven analytics for farm and business optimization
    4. Business services for veteran-owned enterprises

Fiber loop connectivity to FNC Headquarters will ensure redundant, high-speed data transfer and operational security.

3. Self-Sufficient Veteran Community

  1. Veterans will build their own homes using locally milled lumber and available materials.
  2. The community will provide for itself through:
    1. Farming (hydroponics, aquaponics, livestock, field)
    2. Renewable energy solutions to minimize costs and dependence
    3. Manufacturing of essential goods (furniture, tools, housing materials)
  3. All community members contribute and support one another in a mission-driven environment.

4. Homeownership for Veterans

  1. Veterans may apply for an FNC-supported home built in their preferred location.
  2. Once approved, the veteran assembles a team of friends or pays fellow veterans to build the home.
  3. FNC provides materials and additional funding to complete the project.
  4. Upon completion, FNC sells the home to the veteran at cost, ensuring affordable homeownership.

5. Business & Entrepreneurship Program

  1. FNC will fund top graduates to start their own veteran-owned businesses.
  2. Areas of focus include:
    1. Construction & trades
    2. IT services & cybersecurity
    3. Sustainable agriculture & food production
    4. Custom manufacturing (woodworking, furniture, tools)
  3. FNC will provide mentorship, business planning, and funding support.
  4. Each business will become part of a national network of veteran-led enterprises.

6. Medical & Psychological Support

  1. A fully staffed medical center will provide:
    1. Routine check-ups and injury care
    2. On-site psychological services & PTSD treatment
    3. Physical rehabilitation and pain management
  2. Veterans will have immediate access to medical care without delays.

Scalability & Long-Term Vision

  • Phase 1: Core Infrastructure & Training Center
  • Establish the first FNC training and housing facility with an on-site modular data center.
  • Recruit high-caliber veterans from special operations, airborne, and combat arms.
  • Build initial housing, training areas, and remote learning center.
  • Phase 2: Expansion & Business Development
  • Launch veteran-owned businesses supported by FNC.
  • Expand housing projects to provide affordable homeownership to more veterans.
  • Develop specialized IT training programs for remote work opportunities.
  • Phase 3: National Scaling
  • Establish additional FNC facilities across the country.
  • Develop a federated network of veteran-led enterprises.
  • Strengthen strategic partnerships with government agencies, private industry, and veteran organizations.

Conclusion

Five Nines Consulting represents a radical shift in veteran transition programs—not just placing veterans into jobs, but creating self-sufficient communities, enabling homeownership, and launching businesses. By selecting the most capable veterans first, we build a strong foundation that will expand to empower thousands of veterans nationwide.

Through a combination of work, study, housing, and entrepreneurship, FNC ensures that veterans are not just prepared for the civilian world—they own their future.
